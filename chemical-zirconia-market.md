<p><p>The chemical zirconia market features two prominent production methods: hydrothermal and precipitation methods. The hydrothermal method involves synthesizing zirconia from zirconium sources under high-pressure and temperature conditions in an aqueous environment. This method yields high-purity zirconia suitable for advanced applications. Conversely, the precipitation method utilizes chemical reactions to form zirconia precipitates, which are then processed into desired forms. This approach is often more cost-effective and suitable for large-scale production, providing versatility in applications ranging from ceramics to catalysis.</p></p>
