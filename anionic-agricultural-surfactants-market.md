<p><p>The anionic agricultural surfactants market is divided into two primary types: synthetic and bio-based. Synthetic surfactants are chemically engineered compounds that enhance the effectiveness of pesticides and fertilizers by improving their spreadability and penetration. In contrast, bio-based surfactants are derived from natural sources, such as plants and agricultural residues, offering a more environmentally friendly option. The choice between synthetic and bio-based surfactants often depends on performance requirements, environmental considerations, and regulatory factors in agricultural practices.</p></p>
