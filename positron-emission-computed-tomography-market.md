<p><p>The Positron Emission Computed Tomography (PET) market is categorized into three primary types: direct imaging, indirect imaging, and alternative imaging. Direct imaging involves the use of advanced detectors to capture gamma photons emitted by positron-electron annihilation directly, providing high-resolution images. Indirect imaging uses scintillation crystals to convert gamma rays into visible light, which is then processed into images, typically offering lower resolution. Alternative imaging technologies may incorporate novel methods or hybrid systems to enhance image quality and diagnostic capability beyond traditional PET approaches.</p></p>
