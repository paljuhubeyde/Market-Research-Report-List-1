<p><p>Two-dimensional chromatography involves two distinct separation techniques to enhance analytical efficiency and resolution. In 2D gas chromatography, volatile compounds are separated using gas chromatography followed by a second separation, often using a different stationary phase, to improve the identification of complex mixtures. In 2D liquid chromatography, various liquid chromatography techniques are combined, allowing for enhanced separation of compounds based on differing chemical properties. Both methods are vital in pharmaceutical, environmental, and food safety applications, offering comprehensive analytical capabilities.</p></p>
